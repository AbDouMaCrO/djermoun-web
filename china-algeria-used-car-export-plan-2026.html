1 · Compliance & Sourcing — The Two Regulatory Gates

The business lives in the overlap of two rulebooks. A car must satisfy both or it's dead inventory.

Gate A — China's export rules (tightened January 1, 2026)

The November 14, 2025 four-ministry circular (MOFCOM, MIIT, Public Security, Customs) took effect January 1, 2026 and targets the "zero-kilometre used car" trade:

  • The 180-day rule: any vehicle registered for less than 180 days at export requires an After-Sales Service Confirmation Letter (售后服务确认书) from the manufacturer — OEM official seal, destination country named, VIN/details, overseas service network. No letter → no export license. OEMs issue these selectively to protect official distributors; BYD is notoriously restrictive because it has an official Algerian distributor.
  • Data matching: the export license application must match the Motor Vehicle Registration Certificate (机动车登记证书) exactly — brand, model, registration date, transfer date. Any mismatch = rejection.
  • Qualified exporter requirement: used cars can only be exported by enterprises holding a used-car export qualification from their provincial commerce department. Individuals work through a licensed export company (autocango and similar platforms sit on this layer) for a service fee of roughly $300–600/car.
Strategic consequence The sweet spot is vehicles registered 6–30 months ago. Over 180 days clears China's gate without OEM cooperation; under 3 years clears Algeria's. That ~2.5-year band is the entire sourcing universe.

Gate B — Algeria's import rules (droit commun, individuals)

  • Age: strictly under 3 years, measured from first registration in China to customs registration at the Algerian port — not purchase date, not shipping date.
  • Fuel: petrol, hybrid, or 100% electric only. Diesel is blocked.
  • Displacement: maximum 1,600 cm³ for petrol/hybrid. No displacement limit for EVs.
  • Importer: resident Algerian individuals only, one vehicle per person every 3 years, paid from the importer's own hard-currency funds.
  • Steering: left-hand drive (Chinese domestic cars are LHD — no issue).

Sourcing checklist — apply before committing a single yuan

  1. First registration date: > 180 days ago AND still < 3 years old on estimated Algerian port arrival (arrival = purchase + ~60–75 days).
  2. Petrol ≤ 1,600cc, or hybrid ≤ 1,600cc, or BEV.
  3. One owner, clean 登记证书 — no liens or mortgage annotations (a mortgaged car cannot get the export transfer registration).
  4. Verify VIN against the registration certificate; odometer and accident history via a 查博士 / 懂车帝 report.
  5. Channels: autocango-type export platforms (aggregated, export-ready), 瓜子/懂车帝 domestic listings (cheaper, agent handles export qualification), dealer trade-in networks in Guangdong/Zhejiang (best prices on 1–2 year EVs).

Critical path warning The 3-year clock runs during the 20–45 day sea transit. Never buy a car that will be older than 2 years 9 months on the estimated arrival date. Port congestion in Algiers is real; the 3-month buffer is not optional.

4 · Profit Optimization — What to Ship

The tax asymmetry does all the work: petrol cars eat a ~50%+ duty stack; 100% EVs get an 80% reduction on total duties and taxes, hybrids a partial reduction — and the state EV purchase subsidy (up to ~$15,000) runs through end-2026.

Tier 2 — Hybrids ≤ 1,600cc

BYD Song Plus DM-i and Qin Plus DM-i (both 1.5L = 1,497cc, under the cap) — partial duty relief, huge brand pull, strong resale. Margins ~$3,500–6,000.

Tier 3 — Small-displacement petrol (only if the buyer insists)

Chery Tiggo 4 Pro 1.5T (1,498cc ✓), Geely GX3 Pro. The ~50% duty stack compresses margins to $1,500–3,000/unit.

Displacement cliff The popular Chery Tiggo 7 Pro's 1.6T is 1,598cc — legal but zero headroom. Verify the exact homologated displacement on the registration certificate before buying; 1,600cc is a hard cliff, not a rounding target.

Avoid entirely

  • Diesel anything; petrol > 1,600cc.
  • Cars registered < 180 days — unless the OEM letter is already in hand (don't count on it from BYD).
  • Low-recognition EV startups: Algerian resale demands BYD/Chery/Geely badge equity and parts availability.
Portfolio recommendation 70% BEV (Seagull / Dolphin / Atto 3) · 25% DM-i hybrids · 5% opportunistic petrol. Batch 3 cars per 40ft container to cut per-unit freight ~30% vs RoRo spot rates.
