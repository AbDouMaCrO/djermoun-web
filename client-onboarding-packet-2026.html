Recitals

WHEREAS Algerian regulations in force permit resident natural persons to import, once every three (3) years and for personal use, one (1) used passenger vehicle less than three (3) years old, powered by petrol, hybrid or electric motorisation, within the applicable displacement limits (the “Import Quota”);

WHEREAS the Client wishes to exercise his/her personal Import Quota and has requested the Agent's assistance in identifying a vehicle in the People's Republic of China and coordinating the logistics and administrative formalities of its importation;

WHEREAS the Agent is a service provider offering sourcing, coordination and administrative facilitation services, and does not sell, import, or hold title to any vehicle under this Agreement; NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

Article 1 — Definitions

1.1 “Vehicle”: the used motor vehicle described in Annex A, identified by its Vehicle Identification Number (VIN): [_________________].

1.2 “Chinese Supplier”: the licensed used-vehicle export enterprise established in the People's Republic of China, identified in Annex B, which sells the Vehicle directly to the Client.

1.3 “Facilitation Services”: exclusively the services enumerated in Article 3.2, rendered in consideration of the Facilitation Fee.

1.4 “Facilitation Fee”: the fixed remuneration owed to the Agent under Article 5, distinct and separate from the Vehicle Price.

1.5 “Vehicle Price”: the purchase price payable by the Client directly to the Chinese Supplier, as stated in the Supplier's commercial invoice.

1.6 “Import Quota”: the Client's personal right under Algerian law to import one used vehicle less than three (3) years old, exercisable once every three (3) years.

1.7 “Sourcing Commencement”: the earlier of (a) the Agent's transmission to the Client of a vehicle proposal matching the specifications in Annex A; or (b) the Agent's engagement of any third party (supplier, inspector, freight forwarder) on the Client's behalf.

Article 2 — Legal Status of the Agent

2.1 The Agent acts solely as a service provider and facilitator (prestataire de services / mandataire) within the meaning of the provisions of the Algerian Civil Code governing mandate, and in no case as seller, buyer, reseller, dealer, distributor, importer, manufacturer, or manufacturer's representative.

2.2 The Agent never acquires, holds, or transfers ownership of the Vehicle. Legal title passes directly from the Chinese Supplier to the Client in accordance with the sale contract and the commercial invoice issued by the Chinese Supplier in the Client's name.

2.3 The Client is and remains at all times the sole importer of record before Algerian Customs, the domiciliation bank and all Algerian authorities. All import documents (bill of lading, commercial invoice, certificate of conformity, customs declaration) shall be established in the Client's name.

2.4 The Agent gives no warranty as to the Vehicle's mechanical condition, hidden defects (vices cachés), conformity or fitness for purpose. Any warranty claim lies exclusively against the Chinese Supplier and/or the manufacturer. The Agent's obligations are obligations of means (obligation de moyens), not of result.

2.5 Nothing in this Agreement creates any partnership, joint venture, employment or commercial agency relationship between the Parties.

Article 3 — Scope of Services

3.1 The Agent's engagement is strictly limited to the Facilitation Services listed in Article 3.2. Any service not expressly listed is excluded.

3.2 The Facilitation Services comprise:

  • identification of one or more vehicles in China matching the specifications in Annex A;
  • verification, on a best-efforts basis, of the vehicle's registration date, displacement, motorisation and documentary compliance with the Import Quota conditions;
  • coordination with the Chinese Supplier for the issuance of export documentation (export licence, inspection certificate, certificate of origin, commercial invoice in the Client's name);
  • coordination of the Certificate of Conformity (COC) inspection and of the ECTN/BSC issuance prior to vessel departure;
  • coordination of maritime freight booking to the Algerian port designated in Annex A;
  • referral of the Client to a licensed customs broker (transitaire) at the port of arrival, it being specified that the transitaire contracts directly with the Client.

3.3 Expressly excluded: payment of the Vehicle Price; payment of customs duties and taxes; customs clearance itself (reserved to licensed transitaires); vehicle registration; any mechanical warranty; and any act reserved by Algerian law to licensed professions.

Article 6 — Client Warranties: Eligibility and Quota

6.1 The Client formally warrants and declares on his/her honour that: (a) he/she is an Algerian resident natural person legally eligible to exercise the Import Quota; (b) he/she has not imported any vehicle under the Import Quota regime during the thirty-six (36) months preceding the expected date of customs clearance; (c) he/she is not subject to any customs, judicial or administrative measure barring the import; (d) all information and documents provided to the Agent are accurate and authentic.

6.2 These warranties constitute an essential and determining condition of the Agent's consent, without which the Agent would not have contracted.

Article 7 — Regulatory Risk Allocation and Hold-Harmless

7.1 Quota rejection — hold harmless. If Algerian Customs refuses clearance of the Vehicle, in whole or in part, on the ground that the Client had previously exhausted his/her Import Quota, or on the ground of any inaccuracy in the warranties of Article 6, then: (a) the Agent shall be fully and irrevocably held harmless and shall bear no liability whatsoever; (b) the Facilitation Fee remains fully acquired by the Agent; (c) all consequences (re-export costs, storage, demurrage, fines, confiscation, loss of the Vehicle Price) are borne exclusively by the Client; (d) the Client shall indemnify the Agent against any third-party claim (supplier, carrier, transitaire) arising from the rejection.

7.2 Regulatory change. Algerian import regulations (age limits, displacement limits, motorisation requirements, duty rates, exemptions) and Chinese export regulations may change at any time. The Agent shall not be liable for the consequences of any regulatory change occurring after the date of signature; costs already incurred remain due.

7.3 Sovereign and administrative acts. The Agent is not liable for decisions of Algerian Customs, the Bank of Algeria, port authorities, Chinese authorities or any administration, nor for vessel delays, port congestion or carrier failures, none of which are within its control.

7.4 Age-limit transit risk. The three (3)-year age limit is assessed at customs clearance and maritime transit time is outside the Agent's control. Provided the Vehicle was, at the date of vessel departure, within the age margin stipulated in Annex A, the risk of the age limit being exceeded due to transit or port delays is borne by the Client (see the Logistics Risk and Shipping Annex).

7.5 Liability cap. In all cases where the Agent's liability is nonetheless established by a final court decision, it shall be capped at the total amount of the Facilitation Fee actually received.

Logistics Risk and Shipping Annex

Annexe Logistique — Risques de Transport Maritime et Délais (Annex D to the Facilitation Agreement)

This Annex is attached to and forms an integral part of Facilitation Agreement No. [______] between [BROKER] (“the Broker”) and [CLIENT] (“the Client”) concerning the vehicle VIN [_________________] (“the Vehicle”). It allocates the risks of maritime carriage between the Chinese port of loading and the Algerian port of discharge.

Article 1 — Definitions

1.1 “Latest Shipment Date” (LSD): the date stipulated in the Completion Sheet by which the Vehicle must be shipped on board, set so that the Vehicle is no older than the Maximum Departure Age of [2 years and 9 months] on the date of vessel departure. 1.2 “Shipped on Board Date”: the date shown on the bill of lading (B/L). 1.3 “Carrier”: the ocean carrier, its agents and any transhipment operator. 1.4 “Transit Period”: the period from vessel departure at the Chinese port to availability of the Vehicle for customs clearance at the Algerian port.

Article 2 — Transit Delays and the Three-Year Rule

2.1 Nature of the Broker's duty. The Broker's sole obligation as to timing is to use best efforts to procure that the Vehicle is shipped on board no later than the LSD and within the Maximum Departure Age. This obligation is conclusively discharged by production of a bill of lading bearing a Shipped on Board Date on or before the LSD.

2.2 Age-out exoneration. The Client acknowledges that Algerian regulations assess the three (3)-year age limit at the date of customs registration, and that the Transit Period is entirely outside the Broker's control. Accordingly, where the Vehicle was shipped on or before the LSD and within the Maximum Departure Age, the Broker shall bear no liability whatsoever if the Vehicle exceeds the three-year limit before customs registration by reason of, without limitation: port congestion at origin, transhipment or destination ports; vessel rerouting or diversion, including diversions of Red Sea / Suez routings via the Cape of Good Hope; adverse weather or sea conditions; carrier schedule changes, blank sailings, vessel substitution or breakdown; strikes, lock-outs or labour actions; quarantine or sanitary measures; or administrative backlog at the Algerian port.

2.3 Informed acceptance. The Client confirms having been informed that typical transit is 20 to 45 days but may extend beyond, and that the LSD incorporates a prudential buffer. The Client approves the LSD and the Maximum Departure Age stated in the Completion Sheet.

2.4 Consequences of age-out. If, despite timely shipment under Article 2.1, the Vehicle ages out of the import window: (a) all consequences (refusal of clearance, re-export costs, storage, demurrage, fines, forced resale abroad) are borne exclusively by the Client; (b) the Facilitation Fee remains fully acquired; (c) at the Client's written request, the Broker may assist, on a best-efforts and separately remunerated basis, with re-export or resale of the Vehicle in a third country.

2.5 For the avoidance of doubt, if the Broker fails to procure shipment by the LSD for reasons attributable to the Broker alone (excluding acts of the Client, the Supplier, the Carrier or any authority), the Client's remedy is limited in accordance with Article 7.5 of the Facilitation Agreement (fee cap).

Article 3 — Damage in Transit; Risk Transfer and Marine Insurance

3.1 Risk transfer. All maritime risk in the Vehicle transfers to the Client no later than the vessel's departure from the Chinese port, or earlier where the applicable Incoterm [______] so provides. The Broker, who holds no title and is not a party to the contract of carriage, bears no transit risk at any time.

3.2 Excluded damage. The Broker shall in no case be liable for: scratches, dents, chips or paint damage; interior soiling or damage; water, humidity or salt-air damage and resulting corrosion; 12V or traction battery discharge during carriage; theft of the Vehicle, its parts, keys or accessories; vandalism; general average contributions; or total loss of the Vehicle, however arising during the Transit Period.

3.3 Mandatory marine cargo insurance. The Client shall ensure that the Vehicle is covered by marine cargo insurance for at least 110% of the sum of the invoice value and freight, either (a) included in the Supplier's CIF terms — in which case the Client must obtain and verify the insurance certificate before departure — or (b) subscribed by the Client directly. Failure to procure or verify insurance is at the Client's sole risk and shall never engage the Broker's liability.

3.4 Claims procedure. Upon delivery at the Algerian port, the Client (or his transitaire) must: record any visible damage on the delivery/survey report (constat) before removing the Vehicle from the port; photograph the damage; and notify the insurer and the Carrier within the applicable time bars (typically three (3) days for apparent damage, without delay for concealed damage). The Broker may, as a courtesy, assist in assembling claim documents, but is never a party to, nor guarantor of, any insurance or carriage claim.

Article 4 — Customs, Demurrage and Mid-Transit Policy Change

4.1 Client-borne charges. The Client bears exclusively and without recourse against the Broker: all customs duties and taxes; port and terminal handling charges; storage and parking fees; container demurrage and detention; transitaire fees; inspection and conformity fees; and any fine or penalty assessed at the Algerian port.

4.2 Prompt clearance. The Client shall complete banking formalities, appoint a transitaire and deposit the clearance file without delay after notice of arrival. All storage and demurrage accruing from the Client's delay, missing documents or unavailability of funds are the Client's sole charge.

4.3 Mid-transit regulatory change. If, after vessel departure, the Algerian authorities suspend, restrict or modify the import regime applicable to the Vehicle (including duty rates, exemptions, age, displacement or motorisation conditions), such that clearance is blocked, delayed or made more onerous: (a) this constitutes an act of public authority (fait du prince) for which the Broker bears no liability; (b) the Facilitation Fee remains fully acquired to the Broker, the Facilitation Services having been rendered; (c) all resulting costs (storage, demurrage, re-export, additional duties) are borne by the Client.

4.4 Seizure attributable to the Client. In case of customs seizure, blocking or litigation arising from the Client's quota status, documents, declarations or funds, Article 7.1 of the Facilitation Agreement (hold-harmless) applies in full.

Article 5 — Maritime Force Majeure

In addition to Article 9 of the Facilitation Agreement, the Parties expressly qualify as force majeure for the purposes of this Annex: acts of war or piracy in transit corridors; closure or restriction of the Suez Canal or of any strait or canal on the routing; embargoes and sanctions affecting the Carrier or routing; closure of the port of loading or discharge; epidemics and quarantine measures; and cyber incidents affecting the Carrier or port systems. Occurrence of such an event suspends the Broker's coordination obligations without liability; sums due for services already rendered remain acquired.

Article 6 — Precedence and Survival

6.1 In case of conflict between this Annex and the Facilitation Agreement on carriage, delay or transit-risk matters, this Annex prevails. 6.2 Articles 2 to 5 survive delivery of the Vehicle and termination of the Facilitation Agreement. 6.3 Governing law and jurisdiction: as per Article 11 of the Facilitation Agreement.

Letter of Authorization and Power of Attorney Mandate

Procuration — Mandat Spécial d'Autorisation (structured for notarization and localization into French / Arabic)

BEFORE Maître [NAME], Notary (Notaire) at [CITY, WILAYA], or by private deed where permitted, THE UNDERSIGNED: Mr./Mrs. [FULL NAME], born on [DATE] at [PLACE], of Algerian nationality, holder of ID/Passport No. [______], resident at [ADDRESS] (hereinafter “the Principal”), HEREBY APPOINTS AS SPECIAL ATTORNEY (mandataire spécial): [BROKER LEGAL NAME], RC No. [______], registered office at [ADDRESS], represented by [NAME] (hereinafter “the Attorney”), in accordance with the provisions of the Algerian Civil Code governing mandate (wakala), for the sole and limited purposes set out below in connection with the importation of the vehicle VIN [_________________] (“the Vehicle”) purchased by the Principal from [CHINESE EXPORTER / AutoCango supplier] (“the Exporter”).

Article 1 — Powers Granted (Strictly Limited)

The Principal authorizes the Attorney, in the Principal's name and on the Principal's account, to:

  • (a) Communicate and correspond with the Exporter and its platform (including AutoCango), the inspection bodies, the freight forwarder and the shipping line, concerning the purchase, inspection, documentation and shipment of the Vehicle;
  • (b) Transmit the Principal's identity details (full name, passport/ID number, address) and copies of the Principal's identity documents to the Exporter, the forwarder and the authorities concerned, solely as required to establish the export file and the transport documents in the Principal's name;
  • (c) Sign and submit, in the Principal's name: shipping bookings and manifests; the application for the Electronic Cargo Tracking Note (ECTN/BSC); applications and appointments for the pre-shipment inspection and the Certificate of Conformity (COC); and the initial Chinese export documentation relating to the Vehicle;
  • (d) Receive on the Principal's behalf the transport and export documents (bill of lading, commercial invoice, certificate of origin, inspection certificates, COC, ECTN/BSC) and forward them to the Principal or to the Principal's designated transitaire.

Article 2 — Express Exclusions

This mandate confers no power to: (a) purchase or sell any vehicle in the Attorney's own name; (b) acquire, hold or transfer ownership of the Vehicle, which passes directly from the Exporter to the Principal; (c) receive, hold or manage any funds of the Principal, the Vehicle Price being paid by the Principal directly to the Exporter; (d) borrow, guarantee or incur any debt in the Principal's name; (e) perform customs clearance in Algeria, reserved to the licensed transitaire appointed by the Principal; (f) sub-delegate all or part of this mandate without the Principal's prior written consent.

Article 3 — Status of the Attorney; No Transfer of Liability

3.1 The Attorney acts as a strict intermediary and facilitator. All acts performed under this mandate are performed in the name and on the account of the Principal, who remains the sole purchaser, sole importer of record and sole owner of the Vehicle.

3.2 This authorization transfers to the Attorney neither ownership of the Vehicle nor any financial liability attaching to the purchase, carriage, insurance or importation of the Vehicle, including the Vehicle Price, freight, insurance premiums, customs duties, taxes and port charges, all of which remain the Principal's exclusive obligations.

3.3 The Principal ratifies in advance all acts lawfully performed by the Attorney within the strict limits of Article 1, and shall indemnify the Attorney for reasonable documented expenses incurred within the mandate, per the Facilitation Agreement No. [______].

Article 4 — Duration and Revocation

4.1 This mandate takes effect on signature and expires automatically on the earlier of: (a) customs registration of the Vehicle in Algeria; (b) [___] months from signature; or (c) written revocation by the Principal notified to the Attorney and to the third parties concerned. 4.2 Revocation shall not affect acts validly performed before notification. 4.3 The mandate is granted intuitu personae and terminates in the cases provided by law.

Article 5 — Governing Law, Language and Legalization

5.1 This mandate is governed by Algerian law; disputes fall under the exclusive jurisdiction of the territorially competent Algerian courts. 5.2 Established in [Arabic and French; courtesy English translation]; the [Arabic] version prevails. 5.3 Practice note: for use before Chinese entities and authorities, this procuration should be notarized in Algeria and then legalized through the consular channel (Ministry of Foreign Affairs, then the Embassy/Consulate of the People's Republic of China) — to be confirmed with the notary, Algeria not applying the Hague Apostille procedure.
